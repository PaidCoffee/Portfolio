Happy Beavers

Project Overview

Happy Beavers is a small business based in Helsinki, Finland, specializing in renting equipment for all kinds of parties and gatherings. Their offerings range from hot tubs and bouncy castles to wheel-of- fortune games and other essentials for a successful eventâ€”whether it's a child's birthday party or a bachelor party.

At the time, an acquaintance of mine was working for the company and mentioned they were having issues with their website's user interface. I was happy to help.

My Role:

Lead UI & UX Designer

Core Issues

  • Outdated user interface
  • Customers having difficulty placing orders
  • Poor search engine rankings
  • Lack of modern, expected website functionality

Technologies Used

Figma, WordPress, and it's plugins like Elementor, Shopify, etc.

Solutions

It's hard to pinpoint just a few core problems, because, without throwing too much shade, the entire website was the problem. It had clearly been built by someone who was a developer first and a designer fifth. (See screenshots of the old website below.)

Without saying, the only viable solution was a complete redesign.

We were under a tight deadline, only about a month or two to design and build the site. Our team consisted of two people: one with no prior experience in UX/UI design, and one working pro bono (that's me) I had to take the lead of the project, which is something I've never done prior. To say the least, it wasn't the most promising start.

Thankfully, we had a professional developer onboard, which meant we could focus entirely on design. However, we had little to no data to guide our design decisions, and no structured plan. Despite the constraints, we got to work.

Even though the business rents equipment for various types of events, children's parties are the most popular. With that in mind, we aimed to create a website that would appeal to parents: playful imagery, soft and cheerful colors, and simple, intuitive navigation.

The site also had to be built in WordPress using a custom theme provided by the developer. Since the staff lacked technical knowledge, the admin interface needed to be as foolproof and easy to update as possible.

One of the most critical challenges was fixing the broken ordering process. On the previous website, many customers had trouble placing orders, leading to frequent phone calls to staff for assistance. This was a massive design flaw, time-consuming for staff and frustrating for users. Worse yet, it likely drove away a significant number of potential customers who gave up and turned to competitors instead.

Results

One month after launch, the business saw a 30% increase in sales, with numbers continuing to rise compared to previous years. The new website also experienced increased traffic, and customer feedback has been overwhelmingly positive. Importantly, support calls related to placing orders have significantly declined.

All in all, this project was a major success, especially given the time constraints and limited resources. See images below.
